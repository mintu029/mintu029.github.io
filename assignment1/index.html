My impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience

This exhibition delves into the design and development of the Walt Disney Concert Hall, highlighting Frank Gehry's collaborative process and the innovative models used to bring this architectural landmark to life. So I was quite impressed by the boldness of thinking and bringing those thoughts to life.

The main goal that the Interactive Experience conveys

The website offers a rich interactive experience to convey the primary goal of the exhibition “Sculpting Harmony: Frank Gehry and the Walt Disney Concert Hall.” Users can explore more than 150 models, sketches, and images related to Frank Gehry’s design process for the Walt Disney Concert Hall. In particular, the website allows interaction with Gehry’s study models, allowing viewers to rotate and zoom in for detailed observations, along with highlights that provide additional information about specific design elements.

The interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time.

The gehry.getty.edu website uses an interactive experience to guide viewers through Frank Gehry's design process in a timeline.


    Explore the Design Stages – Users can navigate through each stage of the Walt Disney Concert Hall development process, from early sketches to more detailed models.
    Interact with architectural models – Blueprints and 3D models can be viewed from multiple angles, allowing viewers to understand how designs have changed over time.
    Multimedia Integration – Video, audio and images help create a linear yet flexible experience, allowing users to explore at their own pace, while capturing the progression of the project.
    Information Highlights – When interacting with models, users can view markers that provide contextual information, helping them understand each design stage sequentially and intuitively.

Overall, the experience simulates the way Gehry and his team developed design ideas over time, while allowing viewers to experience the evolution of architecture in a coherent and engaging flow.

Experience references to other forms of media

Digital Media

  • Interactive Website – Presents content through a digital interface, allowing users to navigate according to their own needs.
  • 3D Model – Allows users to rotate, zoom and explore the architecture in a visual way.
  • Documentary Videos – Interviews with Frank Gehry and related footage provide deeper context, and give us more information about the exhibition or what he is working on.
  • Sound & Music – Combining orchestral music and sounds from the Walt Disney Concert Hall to enhance the experience, and that was what surprised me the most when I first visited the site.

Traditional media

  • Architectural Drawings & Hand Sketches – Gehry's early designs help to showcase the creative process, and add a sense of surprise to the viewer.
  • Photography – Images of the project in different stages of construction help connect the story.
  • Documentation & Citations – Text passages and annotations provide historical information, much like a traditional exhibition book, giving viewers additional information and understanding of what the author is trying to convey.

Other reference media

  • Gamification – The way users interact with 3D models is similar to VR/AR applications or architectural games.
  • Virtual Museum – The website simulates the approach of digital museums, where users can freely explore the works.
  • Education Apps – Reminiscent of online learning platforms like Google Arts & Culture, where users can access in-depth information about art and design.

This experience is a combination of digital media (website, 3D models, video, audio) and traditional media (drawings, photography, architectural documents) to create a flexible, informative and highly interactive journey of discovery that allows viewers to still immerse themselves in the works.

The experiential actions I learned from this reference

Guide to research approach

  • Timeline Discovery – The experience is designed to allow users to explore information in a progressive manner. This emphasizes the importance of approaching research in a structured way, from the ideation stage to the creation of a finished product.
  • Focus on engagement – Rather than just passively receiving information, the experience encourages users to participate, explore patterns and materials. This suggests that in research, we should actively explore, practice discovering new experiences and analyze instead of just reading documents.

Methods of organizing and presenting research

  • Incorporate multiple media – We can try to combine multiple experiences such as using hand drawings, 3D models, videos, interviews, and audio to convey information. This shows that research should have a diverse approach, not only relying on text but also incorporating images, data, and even audio or visual models.
  • Detail each section – Each model or drawing has additional information to help the viewer understand it better. This emphasizes the importance of providing context and explanation in research, rather than simply presenting facts.

How to act during the research process

  • Engage with multiple sources – Don’t just rely on one source. Combine historical documents, empirical analysis, numerical data, and visual information to get a comprehensive view. It’s essential to enrich your research.
  • Learn from experts & real-world feedback – The site features interviews with Frank Gehry, showing that the research comes not just from books but also from expert conversations and real-world analysis. This is also a fun way to get a closer look.
  • Practical application & testing of ideas – The fact that users can rotate and observe 3D models suggests that research is not just theoretical, but also requires practical testing (e.g. creating models, testing AI, collecting real data).

So when engaging in a research experience, we should explore in a structured way, use multiple interactive methods, diversify our sources, and combine practice with theory for a more comprehensive and in-depth view.

The experiential feel I learned from this reference

Referencing Frank Gehry’s design process for the Walt Disney Concert Hall conveys that engaging in research involves having a clear goal, understanding the purpose of the project, and understanding how to work as a team. Combining hands-on craftsmanship with cutting-edge technology, research requires flexibility and creativity. It is not just an individual effort, but a collective effort. It teaches us to adapt to constraints and balance aesthetic goals and encourages a mindset of continuous learning.
